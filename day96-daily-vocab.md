# Day 96 - Daily Vocabulary

| Word         | Definition                                   | Example 1                                                              | Example 2                                                                  | Example 3                                                                    |
| :----------- | :------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ubiquitous** | Present, appearing, or found everywhere.     | Smartphones are **ubiquitous** in modern society.                       | The internet has become a **ubiquitous** part of daily life.               | Coffee shops are **ubiquitous** in this city.                                |
| **Ephemeral**  | Lasting for a very short time.               | Fashion trends are often **ephemeral**.                                | The beauty of a sunset is **ephemeral**.                                   | His fame was **ephemeral**, lasting only a few months.                       |
| **Meticulous** | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | She is **meticulous** in her work, checking every detail.                | The artist was **meticulous** in his brushstrokes.                          | A **meticulous** researcher leaves no stone unturned.                        |
| **Cacophony**  | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds.       | The city street was filled with a **cacophony** of horns and sirens.   | The orchestra produced a **cacophony** before the conductor began.         | I woke up to the **cacophony** of birds outside my window.                   |
| **Benevolent** | Well meaning and kindly.                     | The **benevolent** donor gave a large sum to charity.                  | Her **benevolent** smile made everyone feel at ease.                       | He was known for his **benevolent** nature and willingness to help.          |
| **Capricious** | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in spring can be very **capricious**.                        | Her **capricious** decisions often led to unexpected outcomes.             | A **capricious** leader can be difficult to follow.                          |
| **Deleterious** | Causing harm or damage.                      | Smoking has many **deleterious** effects on health.                     | The drought had a **deleterious** impact on crop yields.                   | Excessive screen time can be **deleterious** to eyesight.                   |
| **Equanimity** | Calmness and composure, especially in a difficult situation. | She faced the crisis with remarkable **equanimity**.                     | Maintaining **equanimity** under pressure is a valuable skill.              | His **equanimity** helped him navigate the turbulent waters of the market.  |
| **Fastidious** | Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail. | He is **fastidious** about his appearance.                               | The chef was **fastidious** about the quality of his ingredients.          | A **fastidious** editor will catch every grammatical error.                  |
| **Gregarious** | Fond of company; sociable.                   | She is a **gregarious** person who loves to socialize.                   | Dolphins are **gregarious** creatures, often seen in large pods.            | His **gregarious** nature made him popular at parties.                       |
| **Ignominious**| Deserving or causing public disgrace or shame. | The team suffered an **ignominious** defeat.                            | He made an **ignominious** exit from the stage after forgetting his lines. | The scandal led to his **ignominious** resignation.                          |
| **Luminous**   | Emitting or reflecting light; shining.       | The moon was **luminous** in the night sky.                             | Her eyes were **luminous** with excitement.                                | The artist used **luminous** colors in his painting.                         |
| **Mellifluous**| (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | Her **mellifluous** voice captivated the audience.                      | The **mellifluous** sound of the cello filled the hall.                    | He spoke in a **mellifluous** tone, calming everyone present.                |
| **Obfuscate**  | Render obscure, unclear, or unintelligible.  | Politicians often **obfuscate** the truth.                               | He tried to **obfuscate** the issue with irrelevant details.               | The complex jargon only served to **obfuscate** the meaning.                 |
| **Pernicious** | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The **pernicious** influence of social media can be detrimental.        | A **pernicious** rumor can destroy a reputation.                            | The company faced a **pernicious** lawsuit.                                  |
| **Quixotic**   | Extremely idealistic; unrealistic and impractical. | His **quixotic** quest to save the world was admirable but futile.      | She had a **quixotic** belief in the inherent goodness of people.          | A **quixotic** plan rarely succeeds in the real world.                       |
| **Recalcitrant**| Having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority or discipline. | The **recalcitrant** student refused to follow the rules.               | The **recalcitrant** donkey refused to move.                               | Dealing with **recalcitrant** employees can be challenging.                  |
| **Sanctimonious**| Making a show of being morally superior to other people. | His **sanctimonious** attitude annoyed everyone.                         | She gave a **sanctimonious** lecture on morality.                          | The politician's **sanctimonious** speech rang hollow.                       |
| **Tenebrous**  | Dark and shadowy.                            | The old castle had many **tenebrous** corridors.                        | A **tenebrous** atmosphere filled the room.                                | The artist used **tenebrous** shades to create a dramatic effect.            |
| **Vacillate**  | Alternate or waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive. | He tends to **vacillate** between two choices.                           | Don't **vacillate** when making important decisions.                       | The committee continued to **vacillate** on the new policy.                  |
