# Day 103: Daily Vocabulary

| Word | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Unprecedented** | Never done or experienced before; without previous example | 1. The recent technological advancements are truly unprecedented. 2. The company achieved unprecedented growth this quarter. 3. Her kindness was unprecedented in its generosity. |
| **Adept** | Highly skilled or proficient at something | 1. He is adept at solving complex mathematical problems. 2. She became adept at playing the piano after years of practice. 3. The chef is adept at creating innovative dishes. |
| **Dwindle** | To gradually decrease or become smaller | 1. The natural resources of the planet are dwindling rapidly. 2. His hopes of winning began to dwindle as time ran out. 3. The crowd started to dwindle after the concert ended. |
| **Mitigate** | To make something less severe, harmful, or painful | 1. Wearing a helmet can mitigate the risk of head injuries. 2. The government implemented policies to mitigate the effects of the recession. 3. They tried to mitigate the damage caused by the storm. |
| **Plethora** | A large or excessive amount of something | 1. The library has a plethora of books on various subjects. 2. There's a plethora of options available for streaming services. 3. The garden produced a plethora of fresh vegetables this season. |
| **Ambiguous** | Unclear; open to multiple interpretations | 1. The politician's statement was ambiguous, leaving many questions unanswered. 2. The ending of the movie was ambiguous, allowing viewers to draw their own conclusions. 3. Her ambiguous response made it difficult to understand her true feelings. |
| **Scrutinize** | To examine very carefully | 1. The detective scrutinized every detail of the crime scene. 2. The committee will scrutinize the budget proposal before approving it. 3. She scrutinized the document for any errors before submission. |
| **Convey** | To communicate or express | 1. The painting conveys a sense of peace and tranquility. 2. He struggled to convey his emotions in words. 3. The ambassador was tasked with conveying the message to the foreign government. |
| **Candid** | Honest and direct, even when uncomfortable | 1. She gave a candid assessment of the project's shortcomings. 2. His candid remarks were appreciated by the team. 3. The journalist asked a candid question about the scandal. |
| **Omit** | To leave out | 1. Please omit any unnecessary details from your report. 2. The recipe instructed to omit the sugar for a healthier version. 3. He accidentally omitted a crucial step in the instructions. |
| **Caliber** | Level of quality or ability | 1. The new employee is of a very high caliber. 2. The university attracts students of exceptional caliber. 3. The team's performance was not up to its usual caliber. |
| **Prevalent** | Widespread or commonly occurring | 1. Social media usage is highly prevalent among young adults. 2. The belief in superstitions is still prevalent in some cultures. 3. This type of illness is more prevalent during the winter months. |
| **Nuance** | A subtle or slight difference in meaning or tone | 1. The artist captured every nuance of light and shadow in the portrait. 2. It's important to understand the cultural nuances when traveling abroad. 3. The lawyer carefully explained the legal nuances of the case. |
| **Detrimental** | Harmful or damaging | 1. Lack of sleep can be detrimental to your health. 2. The new policy had a detrimental effect on small businesses. 3. His negative attitude was detrimental to team morale. |
| **Discrepancy** | A difference between two things that should match | 1. There was a discrepancy between the witness's statements. 2. The audit revealed a significant discrepancy in the financial records. 3. The discrepancy in the data raised concerns among researchers. |
| **Pivotal** | Critically important or central | 1. The invention of the internet was a pivotal moment in human history. 2. Her decision played a pivotal role in the outcome of the negotiations. 3. The coach's speech was pivotal in motivating the team to win. |
| **Refute** | To prove something is false or incorrect | 1. She presented evidence to refute the accusations against her. 2. The scientist published a paper to refute the prevailing theory. 3. He tried to refute the claims made by his opponent during the debate. |
| **Resilient** | Able to recover quickly from difficulties; strong and adaptable | 1. Despite facing many challenges, the community proved to be incredibly resilient. 2. The resilient plant managed to grow in harsh conditions. 3. She has a resilient spirit that helps her overcome adversity. |
| **Unleash** | To release something powerful or intense | 1. The storm unleashed its fury on the coastal town. 2. The new marketing campaign aims to unleash a wave of consumer interest. 3. The artist's performance unleashed a torrent of emotions in the audience. |
| **Pinnacle** | The highest or most successful point of something | 1. Reaching the summit of Mount Everest was the pinnacle of his climbing career. 2. The company's new product represents the pinnacle of technological innovation. 3. Winning the Olympic gold medal was the pinnacle of her athletic achievements. |
