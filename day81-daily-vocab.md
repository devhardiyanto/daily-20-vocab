# Day 81: Daily English Vocabulary

| Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Saccharine** | Adjective | Excessively sweet or sentimental. | The movie's **saccharine** ending felt forced and unrealistic to many critics. | I find her **saccharine** smile a bit insincere at times. | The dessert was so **saccharine** that I could only eat a few bites. |
| **Sacrosanct** | Adjective | Regarded as too important or valuable to be interfered with. | In many cultures, the family unit is considered **sacrosanct**. | The freedom of the press is a **sacrosanct** right in a democratic society. | To him, his Sunday morning routine was **sacrosanct** and never to be disturbed. |
| **Sagacious** | Adjective | Having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; shrewd. | The **sagacious** leader knew exactly how to handle the crisis. | Her **sagacious** advice helped me navigate the difficult situation at work. | He is known for his **sagacious** investments that have yielded high returns. |
| **Salient** | Adjective | Most noticeable or important. | The **salient** points of the report were summarized in the first two pages. | She highlighted the **salient** features of the new software during the presentation. | The most **salient** difference between the two products is their price. |
| **Salubrious** | Adjective | Health-giving; healthy. | The mountain air is known for its **salubrious** effects on one's well-being. | They moved to a more **salubrious** climate to improve his health. | The gym offers a variety of **salubrious** activities for people of all ages. |
| **Sanctimonious** | Adjective | Making a show of being morally superior to other people. | I can't stand his **sanctimonious** attitude whenever we discuss politics. | She gave a **sanctimonious** speech about the importance of charity, yet she never donated herself. | His **sanctimonious** remarks about other people's lifestyles were quite off-putting. |
| **Sanguine** | Adjective | Optimistic or positive, especially in an apparently bad or difficult situation. | Despite the setbacks, he remained **sanguine** about the project's success. | She has a **sanguine** disposition and always looks on the bright side of things. | The team is **sanguine** about their chances of winning the championship. |
| **Satiate** | Verb | To satisfy (a desire or an appetite) to the full. | The large meal was enough to **satiate** even the hungriest of guests. | He read for hours to **satiate** his curiosity about the historical event. | Nothing seemed to **satiate** her thirst for knowledge and adventure. |
| **Scanty** | Adjective | Small or insufficient in quantity or amount. | The evidence against the suspect was **scanty** and circumstantial. | They had to survive on **scanty** rations during their long journey across the desert. | The report provided only **scanty** details about the proposed changes. |
| **Scrupulous** | Adjective | (Of a person or process) diligent, thorough, and extremely attentive to details. | The accountant was **scrupulous** in keeping track of every penny. | She is **scrupulous** about following all the safety regulations in the laboratory. | The research was conducted with **scrupulous** attention to scientific accuracy. |
| **Scrutinize** | Verb | To examine or inspect closely and thoroughly. | The lawyer **scrutinize**d every word of the contract before signing it. | The customs officer **scrutinize**d the passenger's passport for any signs of forgery. | Scientists continue to **scrutinize** the data to find any potential errors. |
| **Scurrilous** | Adjective | Making or spreading scandalous claims about someone with the intention of damaging their reputation. | The politician was the victim of a **scurrilous** attack by his opponents. | She refused to listen to the **scurrilous** rumors that were circulating about her friend. | The magazine was sued for publishing **scurrilous** allegations against the celebrity. |
| **Seamless** | Adjective | Smooth and continuous, with no apparent gaps or spaces between one part and the next. | The transition between the two scenes in the movie was **seamless**. | The new software provides a **seamless** experience for users across different devices. | The team worked together in a **seamless** manner to complete the project on time. |
| **Sedentary** | Adjective | (Of a person) tending to spend much time seated; somewhat inactive. | A **sedentary** lifestyle can lead to various health problems, such as obesity. | He has a **sedentary** job that requires him to sit at a desk all day. | It's important to incorporate regular exercise into your routine if you have a **sedentary** habit. |
| **Sedition** | Noun | Conduct or speech inciting people to rebel against the authority of a state or monarch. | The group was charged with **sedition** for their role in organizing the protest. | The government took strict measures to suppress any acts of **sedition**. | His writings were considered to be **sedition** and were banned by the authorities. |
| **Sedulous** | Adjective | (Of a person or action) showing dedication and diligence. | He was **sedulous** in his efforts to learn the new language. | Her **sedulous** attention to detail made her an excellent editor. | The team's **sedulous** work resulted in a highly successful product launch. |
| **Sensory** | Adjective | Relating to sensation or the physical senses; transmitted or perceived by the senses. | The museum offers a **sensory** experience that engages all five senses. | The brain processes **sensory** information from the environment to create our perception of reality. | The child's **sensory** development is crucial for their overall growth and learning. |
| **Sensuous** | Adjective | Relating to or affecting the senses rather than the intellect. | The poem was filled with **sensuous** imagery that brought the scene to life. | She enjoyed the **sensuous** pleasure of a warm bath after a long day. | The music had a **sensuous** quality that captivated the audience. |
| **Sententious** | Adjective | Given to moralizing in a pompous or affected manner. | His **sententious** remarks about the importance of hard work were quite annoying. | The book was criticized for its **sententious** tone and lack of original ideas. | She has a tendency to be **sententious** when giving advice to others. |
| **Serendipity** | Noun | The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way. | It was pure **serendipity** that I ran into my old friend at the airport. | Many great scientific discoveries have been the result of **serendipity**. | They found their dream home through a stroke of **serendipity**. |
