# Daily Vocabulary - Day 121

| No. | Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | Mobile phones are now ubiquitous, found in nearly every pocket. | The brand's logo is ubiquitous, appearing on billboards and TV ads alike. | In the digital age, data is ubiquitous, generated by every click and interaction. |
| 2 | **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The ephemeral beauty of cherry blossoms is a highlight of spring. | Childhood is often seen as an ephemeral period, quickly passing into adulthood. | Fashion trends are ephemeral; what's popular today may be forgotten tomorrow. |
| 3 | **Sycophant** | Noun | A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important to gain advantage. | The manager was surrounded by sycophants who constantly praised his mediocre ideas. | No one respects a sycophant; genuine admiration is always preferred. | He was accused of being a sycophant, always agreeing with the boss to get ahead. |
| 4 | **Pernicious** | Adjective | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The pernicious rumors slowly eroded her reputation. | The pernicious influence of social media can be detrimental to mental health. | A pernicious weed can quickly take over a garden if not controlled. |
| 5 | **Quixotic** | Adjective | Extremely idealistic, unrealistic, and impractical. | His quixotic quest to end all wars was admirable but ultimately futile. | She had a quixotic belief that she could change the world with a single act of kindness. | The startup's quixotic business plan failed to attract serious investors. |
| 6 | **Ineffable** | Adjective | Too great or extreme to be expressed in words. | The ineffable joy of holding her newborn child brought tears to her eyes. | The artist tried to capture the ineffable beauty of the sunset on canvas. | There's an ineffable quality to true love that words can't fully describe. |
| 7 | **Disparate** | Adjective | Essentially different in kind, not allowing comparison. | The team consisted of individuals with disparate skills, yet they worked well together. | The disparate elements of the collage somehow formed a cohesive image. | He tried to reconcile the disparate viewpoints of the two opposing factions. |
| 8 | **Lethargic** | Adjective | Affected by lethargy; sluggish and apathetic. | After a large meal, I often feel lethargic and just want to nap. | The hot, humid weather made everyone feel lethargic and unmotivated. | The economy remained lethargic, showing little sign of growth. |
| 9 | **Vociferous** | Adjective | Expressing feelings or opinions in a very loud or forceful way. | The vociferous crowd protested the new policy outside the city hall. | Her vociferous objections to the proposal were heard by everyone in the room. | The vociferous debate continued late into the night. |
| 10 | **Ostentatious** | Adjective | Characterized by vulgar or pretentious display designed to impress. | His ostentatious display of wealth included a fleet of luxury cars. | She found his ostentatious jewelry to be rather tasteless. | The politician's ostentatious promises rarely translated into action. |
| 11 | **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site was a cacophony of drills, hammers, and shouting. | The school band's first practice was a cacophony of mismatched instruments. | I woke up to a cacophony of birds chirping loudly outside my window. |
| 12 | **Loquacious** | Adjective | Very talkative. | The loquacious tour guide shared endless stories about the city's history. | She became quite loquacious after a few glasses of wine. | His loquacious nature made him a popular guest at parties. |
| 13 | **Sublime** | Adjective | Of such excellence, grandeur, or beauty as to inspire great admiration or awe. | The view from the mountain peak was truly sublime. | The artist's work achieved a sublime level of emotional depth. | He found sublime comfort in the quiet solitude of the forest. |
| 14 | **Meticulous** | Adjective | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | The watchmaker was meticulous in assembling the tiny gears and springs. | Her meticulous research ensured the accuracy of her historical account. | He approached every task with meticulous care, leaving no room for error. |
| 15 | **Juxtapose** | Verb | To place two things side by side for comparison or contrast. | The photographer chose to juxtapose images of poverty and wealth in his exhibition. | The play juxtaposes moments of intense drama with lighthearted comedy. | By juxtaposing the old and new designs, the architect highlighted the evolution of style. |
| 16 | **Altruistic** | Adjective | Showing a selfless concern for the well-being of others. | Her altruistic efforts to help the homeless were widely praised. | He made an altruistic decision to donate a portion of his salary to charity. | True altruistic acts are performed without expectation of reward. |
| 17 | **Plausible** | Adjective | Seeming reasonable or probable. | The detective found the suspect's alibi to be plausible. | Her explanation for being late sounded plausible, so I accepted it. | It's a plausible theory, but we need more evidence to confirm it. |
| 18 | **Tantamount** | Adjective | Equivalent in seriousness to; virtually the same as. | His refusal to cooperate was tantamount to an admission of guilt. | Ignoring the safety regulations is tantamount to risking lives. | For many, a lack of internet access is tantamount to social exclusion. |
| 19 | **Aplomb** | Noun | Self-confidence or assurance, especially when in a demanding situation. | She handled the difficult questions from the press with remarkable aplomb. | He delivered his presentation with such aplomb that no one suspected his nervousness. | Despite the unexpected challenge, the pilot landed the plane with great aplomb. |
| 20 | **Rhetoric** | Noun | The art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing. | The politician's fiery rhetoric stirred the emotions of the crowd. | His speech was full of empty rhetoric, lacking any concrete proposals. | The lawyer used powerful rhetoric to sway the jury in his favor. |
