# Daily English Vocabulary - Day 94

Here are 20 advanced English vocabulary words with their meanings and contextual examples.

| Word         | Part of Speech | Meaning                                     | Example 1                                                              | Example 2                                                                  | Example 3                                                                  |
| :----------- | :------------- | :------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Unprecedented | Adjective      | Never done or experienced before; without previous example | The pandemic caused an **unprecedented** shift to online learning.       | The company achieved **unprecedented** growth this quarter.                | Her success in the competition was **unprecedented**.                      |
| Adept        | Adjective      | Highly skilled or proficient at something   | She’s **adept** at explaining complex grammar in a simple way.           | He is **adept** at problem-solving.                                        | The team is **adept** at adapting to new challenges.                       |
| Dwindle      | Verb           | To gradually decrease or become smaller     | Attendance at the evening class **dwindled** after the holidays.         | Their hopes of winning began to **dwindle**.                               | The resources will **dwindle** if we don't conserve them.                  |
| Mitigate     | Verb           | To make something less severe, harmful, or painful | Wearing a seatbelt can **mitigate** the risk of injury in an accident.   | They are trying to **mitigate** the effects of climate change.             | New policies were introduced to **mitigate** the economic impact.          |
| Plethora     | Noun           | A large or excessive amount of something    | Espresso English offers a **plethora** of lessons, courses, and e-books. | The library has a **plethora** of books on various subjects.               | There was a **plethora** of information available online.                  |
| Ambiguous    | Adjective      | Unclear; open to multiple interpretations   | The instructions were **ambiguous**, so no one knew what to do.          | His response was deliberately **ambiguous**.                               | The ending of the movie was **ambiguous**.                                 |
| Scrutinize   | Verb           | To examine very carefully                   | Investors will **scrutinize** the company’s financial statements.        | The detective began to **scrutinize** the evidence.                        | She **scrutinized** his face for any sign of deception.                    |
| Convey       | Verb           | To communicate or express                   | It’s hard to **convey** sarcasm over text.                               | Words often fail to **convey** the depth of emotion.                       | He tried to **convey** his message through gestures.                       |
| Candid       | Adjective      | Honest and direct, even when uncomfortable  | She gave a **candid** answer about her experience with failure.          | Let me be **candid** with you about the situation.                         | His **candid** remarks surprised everyone.                                 |
| Omit         | Verb           | To leave out                                | You can **omit** that paragraph — it’s not essential.                    | Please **omit** any unnecessary details from the report.                   | They decided to **omit** her name from the list.                           |
| Caliber      | Noun           | Level of quality or ability                 | They’re hiring someone of the highest **caliber** for the role.          | The orchestra is known for its high **caliber** musicians.                 | He demonstrated a rare **caliber** of leadership.                          |
| Prevalent    | Adjective      | Widespread or commonly occurring            | Work-from-home policies have become more **prevalent**.                  | The use of smartphones is **prevalent** among young people.                | This disease is more **prevalent** in certain regions.                     |
| Nuance       | Noun           | A subtle or slight difference in meaning or tone | His performance captured every **nuance** of the character.               | She understood the subtle **nuances** of the language.                     | The artist paid attention to every **nuance** of color.                     |
| Detrimental  | Adjective      | Harmful or damaging                         | Smoking is clearly **detrimental** to your health.                       | The lack of sleep can be **detrimental** to your performance.              | His decision proved **detrimental** to the company's reputation.           |
| Discrepancy  | Noun           | A difference between two things that should match | There’s a **discrepancy** between the reports and the receipts.          | The audit revealed a significant **discrepancy** in the accounts.          | We need to investigate the **discrepancy** in the data.                    |
| Pivotal      | Adjective      | Critically important or central             | That meeting was a **pivotal** moment in the negotiation.                | Her role in the project was absolutely **pivotal**.                        | The invention of the wheel was a **pivotal** development in human history. |
| Refute       | Verb           | To prove something is false or incorrect    | She **refuted** the claim with solid evidence.                           | It is difficult to **refute** such strong arguments.                       | He was unable to **refute** the accusations.                               |
| Resilient    | Adjective      | Able to recover quickly from difficulties; strong and adaptable | Despite many setbacks, she remained **resilient** and kept working toward her goal. | The community proved remarkably **resilient** after the disaster.         | Bamboo is a very **resilient** material.                                   |
| Unleash      | Verb           | To release something powerful or intense    | The coach’s speech **unleashed** a wave of motivation in the team.       | The storm **unleashed** its fury on the coastal town.                      | The new policy will **unleash** economic growth.                           |
| Pinnacle     | Noun           | The highest or most successful point of something | Winning the championship was the **pinnacle** of his career.               | He reached the **pinnacle** of his profession.                             | The mountain peak is the **pinnacle** of the range.                        |
