# Day 14 - Daily Vocabulary

| Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the sunset was **ephemeral**, fading within minutes. | Fashion trends are often **ephemeral**, changing from season to season. | She captured the **ephemeral** moment with a quick photograph. |
| **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | In the modern world, smartphones have become truly **ubiquitous**. | The company's logo is **ubiquitous** across all social media platforms. | His influence is **ubiquitous** in the field of quantum physics. |
| **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | The singer's **mellifluous** voice captivated the entire audience. | He delivered the speech in a smooth, **mellifluous** tone. | The sound of the harp was surprisingly **mellifluous**. |
| **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site was a **cacophony** of drills and shouting. | The orchestra tuning up created a brief **cacophony** before the concert began. | I couldn't concentrate with the **cacophony** of traffic outside. |
| **Perfunctory** | Adjective | (Of an action or gesture) carried out with a minimum of effort or reflection. | He gave a **perfunctory** nod to acknowledge the new employee. | The security check was so **perfunctory** that I barely noticed it. | She offered a **perfunctory** apology that lacked any real sincerity. |
| **Fastidious** | Adjective | Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail. | The chef was **fastidious** about the presentation of every dish. | He is a **fastidious** collector who only buys items in mint condition. | Her **fastidious** nature made her an excellent proofreader. |
| **Gregarious** | Adjective | (Of a person) fond of company; sociable. | As a naturally **gregarious** person, she loves hosting large parties. | Lions are not solitary animals; they are highly **gregarious**. | The new student was surprisingly **gregarious** and made friends quickly. |
| **Salient** | Adjective | Most noticeable or important. | The **salient** feature of the new design is its energy efficiency. | He summarized the **salient** points of the long report in a single page. | The mountain's **salient** peak was visible from miles away. |
| **Eschew** | Verb | Deliberately avoid using; abstain from. | They were advised to **eschew** processed foods for a healthier diet. | The politician decided to **eschew** traditional campaign methods. | To maintain a clear mind, he chose to **eschew** all forms of social media. |
| **Pervasive** | Adjective | (Especially of an unwelcome influence or physical effect) spreading widely throughout an area or a group of people. | The smell of burnt toast was **pervasive** throughout the entire building. | A sense of optimism became **pervasive** after the team's victory. | Corruption is a **pervasive** problem in the country's bureaucracy. |
| **Mitigate** | Verb | Make less severe, serious, or painful. | Emergency funds were released to **mitigate** the effects of the drought. | Wearing a helmet can **mitigate** the risk of serious head injury. | They are trying to **mitigate** the environmental impact of the new factory. |
| **Alacrity** | Noun | Brisk and cheerful readiness. | She accepted the new assignment with surprising **alacrity**. | The waiter served the customers with great **alacrity** and a smile. | The team responded to the crisis with speed and **alacrity**. |
| **Profligate** | Adjective | Recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources. | The company was criticized for its **profligate** spending on luxury offices. | He was known for his **profligate** lifestyle, quickly running through his inheritance. | The government's **profligate** use of public funds caused a scandal. |
| **Venerable** | Adjective | Accorded a great deal of respect, especially because of age, wisdom, or character. | The **venerable** professor had been teaching at the university for fifty years. | We visited the **venerable** ancient temple on the hillside. | The institution has a **venerable** history dating back to the 18th century. |
| **Inchoate** | Adjective | Just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary. | The project is still in an **inchoate** stage, with only a few ideas sketched out. | He had an **inchoate** idea for a novel, but the plot was still vague. | The newly formed government was still **inchoate** and struggling to find its footing. |
| **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**. | Her **capricious** decisions made it difficult for her staff to plan ahead. | He was tired of his friend's **capricious** nature and unpredictable outbursts. |
| **Austerity** | Noun | Sternness or severity of manner or attitude; extreme plainness and simplicity of life. | The monks lived a life of strict **austerity** and self-denial. | The government imposed a period of economic **austerity** to reduce the national debt. | The room was decorated with a deliberate **austerity**, featuring only essential furniture. |
| **Vacillate** | Verb | Alternate or waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive. | He tends to **vacillate** between two different career paths. | The committee continued to **vacillate** on the final decision for weeks. | Stop **vacillating** and choose a color for the living room walls. |
| **Sanctimonious** | Adjective | Making a show of being morally superior to other people. | The politician's **sanctimonious** speech about virtue rang hollow to many voters. | I dislike his **sanctimonious** attitude whenever the topic of charity comes up. | She gave a **sanctimonious** lecture on the importance of punctuality, despite being late herself. |
| **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The villain's **nefarious** plot to steal the crown jewels was foiled. | The police uncovered a **nefarious** scheme involving identity theft and fraud. | He was arrested for his **nefarious** activities in the dark web. |
