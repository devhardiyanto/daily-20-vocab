# Daily Vocabulary - Day 02

This list contains 20 English vocabulary words with their definitions and three contextual examples to aid in understanding and usage.

| No. | Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the cherry blossoms is **ephemeral**, lasting only a few weeks each spring. | His fame as a child actor proved to be **ephemeral**, fading away by the time he reached adulthood. | We should cherish these **ephemeral** moments of peace before the storm begins. |
| 2 | **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | In the modern world, smartphones have become **ubiquitous**. | The company's logo is now **ubiquitous** across all major cities. | The scent of pine needles was **ubiquitous** in the mountain air. |
| 3 | **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | The singer's **mellifluous** voice captivated the entire audience. | He delivered the speech in a smooth, **mellifluous** tone that was easy to listen to. | The sound of the harp was **mellifluous** and calming. |
| 4 | **Serendipity** | Noun | The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way. | Meeting my old friend in a foreign country was a moment of pure **serendipity**. | The discovery of penicillin was a classic example of scientific **serendipity**. | She credits **serendipity** for her successful career path. |
| 5 | **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site was a **cacophony** of drills, hammers, and shouting. | The orchestra tuning up created a brief **cacophony** before the conductor raised his baton. | I couldn't concentrate with the **cacophony** of traffic outside my window. |
| 6 | **Pernicious** | Adjective | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The **pernicious** influence of social media on young minds is a growing concern. | He suffered from a **pernicious** disease that slowly weakened his body. | We must fight against the **pernicious** rumors that are damaging her reputation. |
| 7 | **Egregious** | Adjective | Outstandingly bad; shocking. | The student made an **egregious** error on the final exam that cost him the grade. | It was an **egregious** violation of the company's ethical code. | The judge described the crime as an **egregious** abuse of power. |
| 8 | **Quixotic** | Adjective | Exceedingly idealistic; unrealistic and impractical. | His **quixotic** quest to save every stray animal in the city was admirable but unsustainable. | The plan to build a self-sustaining colony on Mars seemed a bit **quixotic**. | She has a **quixotic** belief in the inherent goodness of all people. |
| 9 | **Plethora** | Noun | A large or excessive amount of something. | The store offers a **plethora** of options for organic produce. | I have a **plethora** of tasks to complete before the deadline. | A **plethora** of evidence supported the prosecution's case. |
| 10 | **Sycophant** | Noun | A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage. | The manager was surrounded by **sycophants** who constantly praised his every decision. | I can't stand working with a **sycophant** who only cares about pleasing the boss. | His constant flattery marked him as a clear **sycophant**. |
| 11 | **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**. | Her **capricious** spending habits often left her short of money. | The investors were wary of the CEO's **capricious** management style. |
| 12 | **Tryst** | Noun | A private, romantic rendezvous between lovers. | They arranged a secret **tryst** at the secluded cafe after work. | The garden gazebo was the location of their first romantic **tryst**. | The novel tells the story of a forbidden **tryst** between two rival families. |
| 13 | **Inchoate** | Adjective | Just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary. | The project is still in an **inchoate** stage, with many details yet to be decided. | He presented an **inchoate** idea that needed significant refinement. | The artist's early sketches show the **inchoate** form of his later masterpiece. |
| 14 | **Salient** | Adjective | Most noticeable or important. | The **salient** feature of the new design is its improved energy efficiency. | She summarized the **salient** points of the report for the executive team. | The most **salient** difference between the two proposals is the cost. |
| 15 | **Wane** | Verb | (Of a state or feeling) decrease in vigor, power, or extent; become weaker. | The moon will **wane** after the full moon until it becomes a new moon. | His enthusiasm for the new job began to **wane** after the first few months. | As the day drew to a close, the light began to **wane**. |
| 16 | **Zephyr** | Noun | A soft gentle breeze. | A gentle **zephyr** cooled the air on the hot summer afternoon. | She felt the soft **zephyr** on her face as she stood by the ocean. | The curtains fluttered in the slight **zephyr** coming through the open window. |
| 17 | **Halcyon** | Adjective | Denoting a period of time in the past that was idyllically happy and peaceful. | She often reminisces about the **halcyon** days of her childhood. | The company enjoyed a **halcyon** period of growth before the market crash. | We are hoping for a return to the **halcyon** economic conditions of the 1990s. |
| 18 | **Languid** | Adjective | (Of a person, manner, or gesture) having or showing a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow and relaxed. | The cat stretched out in a **languid** manner, enjoying the sun. | He gave a **languid** wave of his hand, indicating his lack of interest. | The hot, humid air made everyone feel **languid** and sleepy. |
| 19 | **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The villain's **nefarious** plot to steal the crown was foiled by the hero. | The government uncovered a **nefarious** scheme to smuggle illegal goods. | He was arrested for his involvement in a **nefarious** cybercrime operation. |
| 20 | **Pensive** | Adjective | Engaged in, involving, or reflecting deep or serious thought. | The young man sat alone on the bench, looking **pensive** and troubled. | She adopted a **pensive** expression as she considered the difficult question. | The painting depicts a woman in a **pensive** pose, gazing out a window. |
