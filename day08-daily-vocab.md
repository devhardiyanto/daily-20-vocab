# Day 08: Daily Vocabulary - 20 Words

This list provides 20 advanced English vocabulary words, each with its definition, part of speech, and three contextual example sentences to aid in comprehension and usage.

| No. | Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Contextual Examples |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | 1. The beauty of the cherry blossoms is **ephemeral**, lasting only a few weeks each spring. <br> 2. | 2 | **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | 1. In the modern world, smartphones have become truly **ubiquitous**. <br> 2. The company's logo is now **ubiquitous** across all forms of media. <br> 3. The smell of pine needles was **ubiquitous** in the mountain air. |
| 3 | **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | 1. The singer's **mellifluous** voice captivated the entire audience. <br> 2. | 4 | **Serendipity** | Noun | The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way. | 1. I love the **serendipity** of browsing in a used bookstore and finding exactly the book I needed. |
| 5 | **Perfunctory** | Adjective | (Of an action or gesture) carried out with a minimum of effort or reflection. | 1. | 12 | **Fastidious** | Adjective | Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail; meticulous. | 1. The chef was **fastidious** about the presentation of every dish. <br> 2. | 16 | **Ignominious** | Adjective | Deserving or causing public disgrace or shame. | 1. The team suffered an **ignominious** defeat in the final match. <br> 2.
