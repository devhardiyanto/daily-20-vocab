# Day 13 - Daily Vocabulary

| Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the cherry blossoms is **ephemeral**, lasting only a few weeks each spring. | His fame as a child actor proved to be **ephemeral**, fading away as he grew older. | We must cherish these moments, as they are **ephemeral** and will soon be gone. |
| **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | The singer's **mellifluous** voice captivated the entire audience. | She spoke in a **mellifluous** tone, making even the most complex instructions sound soothing. | The sound of the harp was **mellifluous**, filling the ancient hall with gentle music. |
| **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | In the modern world, smartphones have become **ubiquitous**. | The company's logo is **ubiquitous** across all major sporting events. | The problem of plastic waste is **ubiquitous**, affecting every corner of the globe. |
| **Serendipity** | Noun | The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way. | Meeting my old friend in a foreign city was a moment of pure **serendipity**. | The discovery of penicillin was a classic example of scientific **serendipity**. | She credits her career success to a combination of hard work and a little bit of **serendipity**. |
| **Pernicious** | Adjective | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The **pernicious** effects of social media on mental health are a growing concern. | He was unaware of the **pernicious** influence his new associates were having on his decisions. | The doctor warned against the **pernicious** habit of smoking. |
| **Fastidious** | Adjective | Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail. | The chef was **fastidious** about the presentation of every dish. | He is a **fastidious** dresser, always ensuring his clothes are perfectly ironed. | Her **fastidious** research methods ensured the accuracy of her findings. |
| **Gregarious** | Adjective | (Of a person) fond of company; sociable. | As a naturally **gregarious** person, she quickly made friends in her new city. | Lions are not solitary animals; they are **gregarious** and live in prides. | His **gregarious** nature made him an excellent host for the party. |
| **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site was a **cacophony** of drills, hammers, and shouting. | The school cafeteria at lunchtime is always a **cacophony** of noise. | He covered his ears to block out the **cacophony** of the city traffic. |
| **Ineffable** | Adjective | Too great or extreme to be expressed or described in words. | The beauty of the sunset was so **ineffable** that she could only stare in silence. | He experienced an **ineffable** sense of peace after meditating for an hour. | The joy of holding her newborn child was an **ineffable** feeling. |
| **Languid** | Adjective | (Of a person, manner, or gesture) having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow and relaxed. | They spent a **languid** afternoon lounging by the pool. | The cat stretched in a **languid** manner before finally getting up. | Her **languid** movements suggested a deep sense of calm and relaxation. |
| **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The villain's **nefarious** plot was foiled by the quick-thinking hero. | The company was involved in a **nefarious** scheme to defraud investors. | He was arrested for his **nefarious** activities in the black market. |
| **Quixotic** | Adjective | Exceedingly idealistic; unrealistic and impractical. | His **quixotic** quest to save every stray animal in the city was admirable but impossible. | The young inventor had a **quixotic** vision for a perpetual motion machine. | She realized her dream of becoming a professional artist was somewhat **quixotic**. |
| **Reticent** | Adjective | Not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily. | He was **reticent** about his past, refusing to discuss his time in the military. | The witness remained **reticent** despite the lawyer's persistent questioning. | Her naturally **reticent** personality made her a good listener. |
| **Sycophant** | Noun | A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage. | The manager was surrounded by **sycophants** who constantly praised his every decision. | She was tired of dealing with the **sycophant** who only complimented her to get a promotion. | No one respected the **sycophant** who always agreed with the boss. |
| **Tantamount** | Adjective | Equivalent in seriousness to; virtually the same as. | His silence on the matter was **tantamount** to an admission of guilt. | Ignoring the safety regulations is **tantamount** to inviting disaster. | For many, a pay cut is **tantamount** to a declaration of war by the company. |
| **Vacillate** | Verb | Alternate or waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive. | He tends to **vacillate** between two different investment strategies. | The committee continued to **vacillate** on the final decision, delaying the project. | Stop **vacillating** and choose a color for the living room walls. |
| **Wistful** | Adjective | Having or showing a feeling of vague or regretful longing. | She gave a **wistful** glance at the old playground where she used to play. | The song evoked a **wistful** nostalgia for his college days. | He had a **wistful** look in his eyes as he spoke of his lost love. |
| **Zealous** | Adjective | Having or showing zeal (great energy or enthusiasm) in pursuit of a cause or objective. | The new employee was incredibly **zealous** and worked late every night. | She is a **zealous** advocate for environmental protection. | His **zealous** commitment to the project inspired the rest of the team. |
| **Juxtaposition** | Noun | The fact of two things being seen or placed close together with contrasting effect. | The artist used the **juxtaposition** of light and shadow to create a dramatic effect. | The film's opening scene featured a striking **juxtaposition** of wealth and poverty. | The **juxtaposition** of the ancient ruins against the modern skyline was fascinating. |
| **Inscrutable** | Adjective | Impossible to understand or interpret. | The man's expression remained **inscrutable** throughout the interrogation. | The ancient text was written in an **inscrutable** script that no one could decipher. | She found the workings of the stock market to be completely **inscrutable**. |
