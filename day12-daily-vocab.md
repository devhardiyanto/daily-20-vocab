# Day 12: Daily 20 Vocabulary Words

| Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the cherry blossoms is **ephemeral**, lasting only a few weeks. | She captured the **ephemeral** moment of the sunset with her camera. | The company's success proved to be **ephemeral** after the market shift. |
| **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | In the modern world, smartphones have become **ubiquitous**. | The brand's logo is **ubiquitous** across all major sporting events. | A sense of fear was **ubiquitous** in the city after the disaster. |
| **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | The singer's **mellifluous** voice charmed the entire audience. | He delivered the speech in a **mellifluous** tone that was easy to listen to. | The sound of the harp was **mellifluous** and calming. |
| **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**. | Her **capricious** spending habits led to financial trouble. | The editor was known for his **capricious** decisions about which articles to publish. |
| **Equanimity** | Noun | Mental calmness, composure, and evenness of temper, especially in a difficult situation. | The pilot maintained his **equanimity** despite the severe turbulence. | Practicing meditation helps to cultivate a sense of **equanimity**. | She faced the criticism with remarkable **equanimity**. |
| **Reticent** | Adjective | Not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily. | He was **reticent** about his past, preferring to keep it private. | The new employee remained **reticent** during the team meeting. | Her **reticent** nature made it difficult to gauge her true feelings. |
| **Alacrity** | Noun | Brisk and cheerful readiness. | She accepted the new project with **alacrity**. | The staff responded to the customer's request with impressive **alacrity**. | Despite the early hour, the students approached the lesson with **alacrity**. |
| **Voracious** | Adjective | Wanting or devouring great quantities of food; having a very eager approach to an activity. | He is a **voracious** reader, finishing several books a week. | The young athlete has a **voracious** appetite for competition. | The fire spread with **voracious** speed through the dry forest. |
| **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site produced a deafening **cacophony** of noise. | The orchestra's warm-up was a brief **cacophony** before the conductor arrived. | The meeting descended into a **cacophony** of shouting and arguments. |
| **Perfunctory** | Adjective | (Of an action or gesture) carried out with a minimum of effort or reflection. | He gave a **perfunctory** nod to acknowledge the greeting. | The security guard conducted a **perfunctory** check of the bags. | Her apology was **perfunctory** and lacked any real sincerity. |
| **Misanthrope** | Noun | A person who dislikes humankind and avoids human society. | The old hermit was a notorious **misanthrope**, living alone in the woods. | His constant complaints about people suggest he might be a **misanthrope**. | Despite his grumpy exterior, the **misanthrope** secretly donated to charity. |
| **Exacerbate** | Verb | To make (a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling) worse. | The new policy will only **exacerbate** the existing tensions. | Scratching the rash will **exacerbate** the irritation. | The lack of rain will **exacerbate** the water shortage in the region. |
| **Pervasive** | Adjective | Spreading widely throughout an area or a group of people. | The influence of social media is **pervasive** in modern culture. | A sense of optimism was **pervasive** among the team members. | The smell of smoke was **pervasive** throughout the entire building. |
| **Ostentatious** | Adjective | Characterized by vulgar or pretentious display; designed to impress or attract notice. | He drove an **ostentatious** sports car to the low-key event. | The wedding reception was criticized for its **ostentatious** decorations. | She wore an **ostentatious** diamond necklace that drew everyone's attention. |
| **Inadvertent** | Adjective | Not resulting from or achieved through deliberate planning. | The error was **inadvertent** and quickly corrected. | He made an **inadvertent** mistake by sending the email to the wrong person. | The spill was an **inadvertent** consequence of his haste. |
| **Mitigate** | Verb | To make less severe, serious, or painful. | They are trying to **mitigate** the effects of climate change. | The new medication will help to **mitigate** the patient's pain. | Insurance can **mitigate** the financial risks associated with natural disasters. |
| **Placid** | Adjective | (Of a person or animal) not easily upset or excited; (of a place or stretch of water) calm and peaceful. | The lake was **placid** and reflected the surrounding mountains perfectly. | She has a remarkably **placid** temperament, rarely getting angry. | The small town offered a **placid** escape from the city's hustle. |
| **Assiduous** | Adjective | Showing great care and perseverance. | The project required **assiduous** attention to detail. | She was an **assiduous** student, always completing her assignments on time. | The team's **assiduous** efforts finally resulted in a breakthrough. |
| **Insipid** | Adjective | Lacking flavor or interest. | The soup was bland and **insipid**, needing more seasoning. | His presentation was so **insipid** that half the audience fell asleep. | The movie was criticized for its **insipid** plot and predictable characters. |
| **Proclivity** | Noun | A tendency to choose or do something regularly; an inclination or predisposition. | He has a **proclivity** for staying up late and working at night. | Her natural **proclivity** for mathematics made the subject easy for her. | The study found a genetic **proclivity** toward certain types of behavior. |
