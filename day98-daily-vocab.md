# Day 98: Daily Vocabulary

| Word          | Definition                                                | Example 1                                                              | Example 2                                                              | Example 3                                                              |
| :------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ubiquitous**  | Present, appearing, or found everywhere.                  | Smartphones are **ubiquitous** in modern society.                       | The company aims to make its product **ubiquitous**.                   | Coffee shops are becoming increasingly **ubiquitous**.                 |
| **Ephemeral**   | Lasting for a very short time.                            | Fashion trends are often **ephemeral**.                                | The beauty of a sunset is **ephemeral**.                               | Childhood memories can be surprisingly **ephemeral**.                  |
| **Serendipity** | The occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way. | Finding that old photo was a moment of pure **serendipity**.           | Their meeting was pure **serendipity**.                                | **Serendipity** played a role in the discovery of penicillin.          |
| **Mellifluous** | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | Her **mellifluous** voice captivated the audience.                     | The **mellifluous** sound of the cello filled the hall.                | He spoke in a **mellifluous** tone, calming everyone present.          |
| **Obfuscate**   | Render obscure, unclear, or unintelligible.               | Politicians often **obfuscate** the truth.                             | He tried to **obfuscate** the issue with irrelevant details.           | The complex jargon only served to **obfuscate** the meaning.           |
| **Pernicious**  | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The **pernicious** effects of social media on mental health.           | He attributes his failure to a **pernicious** influence.               | The **pernicious** rumor spread quickly.                               |
| **Querulous**   | Complaining in a petulant or whining manner.              | The child became **querulous** when he didn't get his way.             | Her voice was **querulous** and irritable.                             | He was tired of her **querulous** complaints.                          |
| **Rancor**      | Bitterness or resentfulness, especially long-standing.    | The debate was marked by **rancor** and personal attacks.              | He spoke with **rancor** about his former employer.                    | There was no **rancor** in her forgiveness.                            |
| **Sagacious**   | Having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; shrewd. | The **sagacious** old man offered wise advice.                          | A **sagacious** decision saved the company from ruin.                  | His **sagacious** observations were always insightful.                 |
| **Tenuous**     | Very weak or slight.                                      | The evidence for his claim is **tenuous**.                             | Their connection remained **tenuous** over the years.                   | The **tenuous** thread of hope began to fray.                          |
| **Ubiquitous**  | Present, appearing, or found everywhere.                  | Smartphones are **ubiquitous** in modern society.                       | The company aims to make its product **ubiquitous**.                   | Coffee shops are becoming increasingly **ubiquitous**.                 |
| **Vacillate**   | Alternate or waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive. | He tends to **vacillate** between two extremes.                        | She **vacillated** for a long time before making a decision.           | Don't **vacillate** when you know what's right.                        |
| **Wary**        | Feeling or showing caution about possible dangers or problems. | Be **wary** of strangers offering gifts.                                | She was **wary** of his sudden friendliness.                           | The cat was **wary** of the new dog.                                   |
| **Zealous**     | Having or showing zeal.                                   | He is a **zealous** supporter of the team.                             | Her **zealous** efforts led to the project's success.                  | The **zealous** activist fought for environmental protection.          |
| **Abstruse**    | Difficult to understand; obscure.                         | The philosopher's writings were often **abstruse**.                    | He found the mathematical concepts too **abstruse**.                   | The poem was filled with **abstruse** metaphors.                       |
| **Banal**       | So lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring.    | The lyrics were rather **banal** and uninspired.                       | He made a few **banal** remarks about the weather.                     | The movie's plot was utterly **banal**.                                |
| **Candid**      | Truthful and straightforward; frank.                      | She gave a **candid** assessment of the situation.                     | To be **candid**, I don't think it's a good idea.                      | He was refreshingly **candid** about his mistakes.                     |
| **Dearth**      | A scarcity or lack of something.                          | There is a **dearth** of good teachers in the area.                    | The report highlighted the **dearth** of affordable housing.           | A **dearth** of evidence made the case difficult to prove.             |
| **Ebullient**   | Cheerful and full of energy.                              | Her **ebullient** personality brightened the room.                     | The team was in an **ebullient** mood after their victory.             | He greeted us with **ebullient** enthusiasm.                           |
| **Fervent**     | Having or displaying a passionate intensity.              | He was a **fervent** believer in social justice.                       | She expressed her **fervent** hope for peace.                          | The crowd gave a **fervent** cheer.                                    |
