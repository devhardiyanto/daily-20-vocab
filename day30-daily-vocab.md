# Day 30 Daily Vocabulary

| Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Abate** | Verb | (Of something perceived as hostile, threatening, or negative) become less intense or widespread. | The storm began to **abate** after several hours of heavy rain. | We waited for the noise to **abate** before continuing our conversation. | The government's new policies helped to **abate** the rising inflation. |
| **Benevolent** | Adjective | Well meaning and kindly. | The **benevolent** old man donated a large portion of his wealth to the local orphanage. | She was known for her **benevolent** nature and willingness to help anyone in need. | The company's **benevolent** fund provides financial assistance to employees in times of crisis. |
| **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**, changing from sun to snow in minutes. | Her **capricious** decisions often left her team confused about the project's direction. | He was a **capricious** leader, frequently altering his mind without any clear reason. |
| **Deference** | Noun | Humble submission and respect. | He addressed the judge with great **deference**, acknowledging the authority of the court. | In many cultures, showing **deference** to elders is a fundamental social value. | She decided to step aside in **deference** to her colleague's expertise on the matter. |
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the sunset was **ephemeral**, fading into darkness within minutes. | Many social media trends are **ephemeral**, disappearing as quickly as they emerge. | He realized that fame is often **ephemeral** and focused on building a lasting legacy. |
| **Frugal** | Adjective | Sparing or economical with regard to money or food. | By being **frugal** and saving every penny, they were eventually able to buy their own home. | She lived a **frugal** lifestyle, preferring simple meals and modest clothing. | The company adopted a **frugal** approach to spending during the economic downturn. |
| **Garrulous** | Adjective | Excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters. | The **garrulous** neighbor kept me at the gate for an hour talking about her garden. | He became quite **garrulous** after a few drinks, sharing stories with everyone in the room. | Her **garrulous** nature made it difficult for others to get a word in during meetings. |
| **Haughty** | Adjective | Arrogantly superior and disdainful. | The **haughty** waiter looked down his nose at the customers who weren't dressed formally. | Her **haughty** attitude made it difficult for her to make friends at the new school. | He gave a **haughty** laugh when I suggested that he might be wrong. |
| **Immutable** | Adjective | Unchanging over time or unable to be changed. | The laws of physics are considered **immutable** principles of the universe. | Despite the passage of time, her love for her family remained **immutable**. | Some people believe that human nature is **immutable**, while others think it can evolve. |
| **Juxtapose** | Verb | Place or deal with close together for contrasting effect. | The exhibition **juxtaposes** modern abstract paintings with classical sculptures. | The documentary **juxtaposes** the lives of the wealthy with those living in poverty. | By **juxtaposing** these two ideas, the author highlights the inherent contradictions. |
| **Kindle** | Verb | Light or set on fire; arouse or inspire (an emotion or feeling). | He used small twigs to **kindle** a fire in the hearth. | The teacher's passion for history helped to **kindle** a similar interest in her students. | Their shared love for music helped to **kindle** a deep and lasting friendship. |
| **Lucid** | Adjective | Expressed clearly; easy to understand. | The professor gave a **lucid** explanation of a very complex scientific theory. | Despite his age, he remained **lucid** and could recall events from his childhood vividly. | She wrote a **lucid** report that outlined the project's goals and challenges. |
| **Meticulous** | Adjective | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | The architect was **meticulous** in his planning, ensuring every measurement was exact. | She is **meticulous** about her appearance, always making sure her clothes are perfectly pressed. | The researcher kept **meticulous** records of every observation made during the experiment. |
| **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The hackers used **nefarious** methods to gain access to the bank's secure database. | The villain's **nefarious** plot to take over the city was eventually foiled by the hero. | He was involved in several **nefarious** schemes that eventually led to his arrest. |
| **Obscure** | Adjective | Not discovered or known about; uncertain. | The origins of the ancient artifact remain **obscure** despite years of research. | He lived an **obscure** life in a small village, far away from the public eye. | The meaning of the poem was so **obscure** that even the critics couldn't agree on it. |
| **Pragmatic** | Adjective | Dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a way that is based on practical rather than theoretical considerations. | She took a **pragmatic** approach to the problem, focusing on what could be achieved immediately. | The leader's **pragmatic** policies helped the country recover from the economic crisis. | He is a **pragmatic** person who doesn't let emotions cloud his judgment. |
| **Quell** | Verb | Put an end to (a rebellion or other disorder), typically by the use of force. | The police were called in to **quell** the riot that had broken out in the city center. | She tried to **quell** her anxiety by taking deep breaths and focusing on the present. | The manager's calm explanation helped to **quell** the fears of the concerned employees. |
| **Reticent** | Adjective | Not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily. | He was **reticent** about his past, rarely speaking about his life before he moved to the city. | She is naturally **reticent**, preferring to listen rather than participate in loud conversations. | The witness was **reticent** to provide any information that might implicate his friends. |
| **Sycophant** | Noun | A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage. | The CEO was surrounded by **sycophants** who never dared to disagree with him. | He was dismissed as a **sycophant** who was only interested in advancing his own career. | Her constant praise of the manager was seen by her colleagues as the behavior of a **sycophant**. |
| **Taciturn** | Adjective | (Of a person) reserved or uncommunicative in speech; saying little. | The old fisherman was a **taciturn** man who preferred the silence of the sea. | He became increasingly **taciturn** as the meeting progressed, eventually stopping speaking altogether. | Her **taciturn** nature was often mistaken for unfriendliness by those who didn't know her. |
