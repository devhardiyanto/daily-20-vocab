# Day 51 Daily Vocabulary

| Word | Type | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aberration** | Noun | A departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome. | The sudden drop in temperature was a seasonal **aberration**. | For a person who is usually so punctual, being late was a strange **aberration**. | The team's poor performance in the last game was considered an **aberration**. |
| **Benevolent** | Adjective | Well meaning and kindly. | The **benevolent** old man donated a large portion of his wealth to charity. | She was known for her **benevolent** nature and willingness to help others. | The company's **benevolent** policies included generous parental leave. |
| **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The **capricious** weather made it difficult to plan an outdoor event. | He is known for his **capricious** nature, often changing his mind at the last minute. | The stock market can be **capricious**, with prices fluctuating wildly. |
| **Deference** | Noun | Humble submission and respect. | He addressed the judge with great **deference**. | In **deference** to her wishes, we decided to postpone the meeting. | The young soldiers showed **deference** to their experienced commander. |
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the sunset was **ephemeral**, lasting only a few minutes. | Fame in the digital age is often **ephemeral** and fleeting. | The morning dew on the grass was **ephemeral**, disappearing as soon as the sun rose. |
| **Fastidious** | Adjective | Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail. | He is **fastidious** about his appearance, always wearing perfectly ironed clothes. | The chef was **fastidious** about the quality of the ingredients he used. | She is a **fastidious** researcher who double-checks every single fact. |
| **Gregarious** | Adjective | (Of a person) fond of company; sociable. | He is a **gregarious** person who loves attending parties and meeting new people. | Dolphins are **gregarious** animals that live in large social groups. | Her **gregarious** nature made her very popular in school. |
| **Haughty** | Adjective | Arrogantly superior and disdainful. | The **haughty** waiter looked down his nose at the customers. | She had a **haughty** manner that made it difficult for others to approach her. | His **haughty** attitude alienated many of his colleagues. |
| **Impetuous** | Adjective | Acting or done quickly and without thought or care. | His **impetuous** decision to quit his job surprised everyone. | She is known for her **impetuous** nature, often buying things she doesn't need. | The **impetuous** youth rushed into the burning building without thinking of the danger. |
| **Jovial** | Adjective | Cheerful and friendly. | The **jovial** host made everyone feel welcome at the party. | He had a **jovial** personality that brightened up the room. | The atmosphere at the festival was **jovial** and full of laughter. |
| **Kindle** | Verb | Light or set on fire; arouse or inspire (an emotion or feeling). | The campfire was **kindled** with small twigs and dry leaves. | Her speech **kindled** a sense of hope in the hearts of the listeners. | The teacher's passion for science **kindled** an interest in the students. |
| **Lethargic** | Adjective | Affected by lethargy; sluggish and apathetic. | I felt **lethargic** after eating a heavy lunch and wanted to take a nap. | The hot weather made everyone feel **lethargic** and unmotivated. | The patient was **lethargic** and had difficulty staying awake. |
| **Meticulous** | Adjective | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | The architect was **meticulous** in his planning of the new building. | She is a **meticulous** cleaner who never misses a spot. | He kept **meticulous** records of all his business transactions. |
| **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The **nefarious** plot to overthrow the government was discovered just in time. | He was known for his **nefarious** activities in the underworld. | The hacker's **nefarious** actions caused a lot of damage to the company's systems. |
| **Obsequious** | Adjective | Obedient or attentive to an excessive or servile degree. | The **obsequious** assistant was always trying to please his boss. | She found his **obsequious** behavior to be quite annoying. | The waiters were **obsequious**, constantly hovering around the table. |
| **Pensive** | Adjective | Engaged in, involving, or reflecting deep or serious thought. | He sat by the window in a **pensive** mood, staring out at the rain. | She had a **pensive** expression on her face as she listened to the music. | The poet's **pensive** nature was reflected in his melancholic verses. |
| **Quell** | Verb | Put an end to (a rebellion or other disorder), typically by the use of force. | The police were called in to **quell** the riot. | Her calm words helped to **quell** his rising anger. | The government took measures to **quell** the public's fears about the economy. |
| **Reticent** | Adjective | Not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily. | He is **reticent** about his personal life and rarely talks about his family. | She was **reticent** to give her opinion until she had all the facts. | The witness was **reticent** and refused to answer many of the lawyer's questions. |
| **Sycophant** | Noun | A person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage. | He was surrounded by **sycophants** who always agreed with everything he said. | She is not a **sycophant**; she will tell you the truth even if it's unpleasant. | The manager was tired of the **sycophants** in his office and wanted honest feedback. |
| **Trepidation** | Noun | A feeling of fear or agitation about something that may happen. | She approached the stage with a sense of **trepidation**. | There was a lot of **trepidation** among the employees about the upcoming layoffs. | He felt a sense of **trepidation** as he entered the dark, abandoned house. |
