# Day 111 Daily Vocabulary

Here are 20 English vocabulary words for today, along with three contextual examples for each.

| No. | Word | Meaning | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ubiquitous** | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society. | The company's logo is ubiquitous, seen on billboards and TV commercials alike. | Coffee shops are ubiquitous in this part of the city. |
| 2 | **Ephemeral** | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of a sunset is ephemeral, fading away in minutes. | Fame in the age of social media can be incredibly ephemeral. | She enjoyed the ephemeral joy of eating a delicious ice cream cone. |
| 3 | **Resilient** | Able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions. | Children are often surprisingly resilient when faced with adversity. | The local economy proved resilient despite the global financial crisis. | He is a resilient athlete who always bounces back from injuries. |
| 4 | **Pragmatic** | Dealing with things sensibly and realistically. | We need a pragmatic approach to solve this complex issue. | Her pragmatic decision saved the company a lot of money. | He is known for his pragmatic leadership style. |
| 5 | **Eloquent** | Fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing. | The speaker delivered an eloquent speech that moved the audience to tears. | She wrote an eloquent letter of resignation. | His eloquent defense of the new policy convinced many skeptics. |
| 6 | **Meticulous** | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | The artist was meticulous in his painting, ensuring every stroke was perfect. | She kept meticulous records of all her business expenses. | The detective conducted a meticulous investigation of the crime scene. |
| 7 | **Tenacious** | Tending to keep a firm hold of something; clinging or adhering closely. | His tenacious grip on the rope saved him from falling. | She is a tenacious researcher who never gives up on finding the truth. | The team's tenacious defense won them the championship game. |
| 8 | **Inevitable** | Certain to happen; unavoidable. | With the dark clouds gathering, rain seemed inevitable. | Change is an inevitable part of life. | The company's bankruptcy was inevitable after years of poor management. |
| 9 | **Ambiguous** | Open to more than one interpretation; having a double meaning. | The politician gave an ambiguous answer to the reporter's question. | The ending of the movie was intentionally ambiguous. | The instructions were too ambiguous for anyone to follow correctly. |
| 10 | **Lucrative** | Producing a great deal of profit. | She turned her hobby into a highly lucrative business. | Real estate can be a lucrative investment if done right. | He was offered a lucrative contract to play for the rival team. |
| 11 | **Prolific** | Producing much fruit or foliage or many offspring; present in large numbers or quantities. | Shakespeare was a prolific writer, authoring numerous plays and sonnets. | The region is known for its prolific apple orchards. | She is a prolific inventor with over fifty patents to her name. |
| 12 | **Diligent** | Having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties. | The diligent student spent hours studying for the final exam. | They made a diligent effort to find the missing documents. | Her diligent work ethic earned her a promotion. |
| 13 | **Candid** | Truthful and straightforward; frank. | Let me be candid with you about your performance. | The documentary offered a candid look at the lives of the rich and famous. | He appreciated her candid feedback on his manuscript. |
| 14 | **Nostalgic** | Characterized by or exhibiting feelings of nostalgia. | Hearing that old song made him feel incredibly nostalgic. | She took a nostalgic trip back to her hometown. | The movie is a nostalgic tribute to the 1980s. |
| 15 | **Obscure** | Not discovered or known about; uncertain. | The origins of the tradition remain obscure. | He found an obscure book in the dusty corner of the library. | The meaning of the poem is somewhat obscure. |
| 16 | **Profound** | Very great or intense; having or showing great knowledge or insight. | The loss of his father had a profound impact on his life. | The philosopher's ideas were truly profound. | She felt a profound sense of gratitude for their help. |
| 17 | **Superfluous** | Unnecessary, especially through being more than enough. | The extra decorations on the cake were superfluous. | He deleted the superfluous paragraphs from his essay. | Asking for her ID seemed superfluous since they already knew her. |
| 18 | **Versatile** | Able to adapt or be adapted to many different functions or activities. | A Swiss Army knife is a very versatile tool. | She is a versatile actress who can play both comedic and dramatic roles. | The new software is highly versatile and can be used for various tasks. |
| 19 | **Zealous** | Having or showing zeal; passionate. | The zealous fans cheered loudly for their favorite team. | He is a zealous advocate for environmental protection. | Her zealous dedication to the project inspired everyone. |
| 20 | **Benevolent** | Well meaning and kindly. | The benevolent king was loved by all his subjects. | She made a benevolent donation to the local orphanage. | His benevolent smile put the nervous child at ease. |
