| Word | Type | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alacrity** | Noun | Brisk and cheerful readiness. | She accepted the invitation with **alacrity**, excited for the event. | The team responded with **alacrity** to the new challenge. | He moved with surprising **alacrity** for someone of his age. |
| **Benevolent** | Adjective | Well meaning and kindly. | The **benevolent** donor provided scholarships for dozens of students. | She had a **benevolent** smile that made everyone feel welcome. | The organization is known for its **benevolent** work in the community. |
| **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**. | Her **capricious** nature made it difficult to plan anything with her. | The stock market can be **capricious**, reacting to the slightest news. |
| **Diligence** | Noun | Careful and persistent work or effort. | His **diligence** in his studies eventually paid off with top grades. | The detective's **diligence** led to the discovery of new evidence. | She approached every task with great **diligence** and professionalism. |
| **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the sunset was **ephemeral**, fading within minutes. | Fame can be **ephemeral**, often disappearing as quickly as it came. | Many digital trends are **ephemeral**, replaced by something new every week. |
| **Frugal** | Adjective | Sparing or economical with regard to money or food. | He led a **frugal** life, saving every penny for his future. | She is very **frugal** with her expenses, always looking for a bargain. | A **frugal** meal of bread and cheese was all they could afford. |
| **Garrulous** | Adjective | Excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters. | The **garrulous** neighbor kept me at the door for an hour. | He became quite **garrulous** after having a few drinks. | She is known for being **garrulous**, often losing track of the main point. |
| **Haughty** | Adjective | Arrogantly superior and disdainful. | The **haughty** waiter looked down his nose at the customers. | She gave a **haughty** laugh when they suggested she might be wrong. | His **haughty** attitude made him very unpopular with his colleagues. |
| **Immutable** | Adjective | Unchanging over time or unable to be changed. | The laws of physics are considered **immutable** principles. | He believed that his destiny was **immutable** and set in stone. | The contract was signed, making the terms **immutable**. |
| **Jovial** | Adjective | Cheerful and friendly. | The **jovial** host made sure everyone had a drink and a snack. | He was in a **jovial** mood after receiving the good news. | The atmosphere at the party was **jovial** and full of laughter. |
| **Lethargic** | Adjective | Affected by lethargy; sluggish and apathetic. | I felt **lethargic** all day after staying up late last night. | The hot weather made everyone feel **lethargic** and tired. | The patient remained **lethargic** for several hours after the surgery. |
| **Meticulous** | Adjective | Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. | The accountant was **meticulous** in keeping the company's records. | She is **meticulous** about her appearance, always looking perfectly groomed. | The restoration of the old painting required **meticulous** work. |
| **Nonchalant** | Adjective | (Of a person or manner) feeling or appearing casually calm and relaxed. | He gave a **nonchalant** shrug when asked about his success. | She remained **nonchalant** even when the fire alarm went off. | His **nonchalant** attitude toward his exams worried his parents. |
| **Opulent** | Adjective | Ostentatiously rich and luxurious or lavish. | The hotel lobby was decorated in an **opulent** style with gold leaf. | They lived an **opulent** lifestyle, traveling the world in private jets. | The feast was **opulent**, featuring the finest delicacies from around the world. |
| **Pragmatic** | Adjective | Dealing with things sensibly and realistically. | We need to take a **pragmatic** approach to solving this problem. | She is a **pragmatic** leader who focuses on results rather than theory. | His decision was based on **pragmatic** considerations rather than emotion. |
| **Resilient** | Adjective | (Of a person or animal) able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions. | The community proved to be **resilient** after the devastating flood. | Children are often more **resilient** than adults in times of change. | She is a **resilient** athlete who never gives up, even after an injury. |
| **Scrupulous** | Adjective | (Of a person or process) diligent, thorough, and extremely attentive to details. | The research was conducted with **scrupulous** attention to accuracy. | He is **scrupulous** about honesty, never telling even a small lie. | The lawyer was **scrupulous** in following all the legal procedures. |
| **Trivial** | Adjective | Of little value or importance. | Don't waste your time worrying about **trivial** matters. | The difference between the two products is **trivial** and hardly noticeable. | It was a **trivial** mistake that didn't affect the final outcome. |
| **Vibrant** | Adjective | Full of energy and enthusiasm. | The city has a **vibrant** nightlife with many clubs and bars. | She gave a **vibrant** performance that captivated the entire audience. | The room was painted in **vibrant** colors that brightened the space. |
| **Zealous** | Adjective | Having or showing zeal; great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause. | The **zealous** supporters cheered loudly for their team. | He is a **zealous** advocate for environmental protection. | She approached her new job with **zealous** enthusiasm. |
