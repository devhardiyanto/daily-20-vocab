# Day 18: Daily 20 Vocabulary Words

| No. | Word | Part of Speech | Definition | Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ephemeral** | Adjective | Lasting for a very short time. | The beauty of the cherry blossoms is **ephemeral**, lasting only a few weeks. | She captured the **ephemeral** moment of the sunset with her camera. | His fame was **ephemeral**; he was a one-hit wonder. |
| 2 | **Ubiquitous** | Adjective | Present, appearing, or found everywhere. | In the modern world, smartphones have become truly **ubiquitous**. | The company's logo is **ubiquitous** across all major cities. | The fear of failure is a **ubiquitous** feeling among high achievers. |
| 3 | **Mellifluous** | Adjective | (Of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear. | The singer's **mellifluous** voice captivated the entire audience. | He delivered the poem in a deep, **mellifluous** tone. | The sound of the stream was a **mellifluous** background to their conversation. |
| 4 | **Cacophony** | Noun | A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds. | The construction site was a **cacophony** of drills and shouting. | The school band's first practice was a painful **cacophony**. | She covered her ears to block out the **cacophony** of the market. |
| 5 | **Nefarious** | Adjective | (Typically of an action or activity) wicked or criminal. | The villain's **nefarious** plot was foiled by the superhero. | The company was involved in a **nefarious** scheme to defraud investors. | He was known for his **nefarious** activities in the underworld. |
| 6 | **Pernicious** | Adjective | Having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way. | The **pernicious** effects of social media on mental health are well-documented. | She warned him about the **pernicious** influence of his new friends. | The weed is so **pernicious** that it can quickly take over a garden. |
| 7 | **Quixotic** | Adjective | Exceedingly idealistic; unrealistic and impractical. | His **quixotic** quest to save the world's oceans single-handedly was admirable but impossible. | The startup's business plan was dismissed as too **quixotic** by investors. | She has a **quixotic** belief in the inherent goodness of all people. |
| 8 | **Surreptitious** | Adjective | Kept secret, especially because it would not be approved of. | They had a **surreptitious** meeting in the dead of night. | He cast a **surreptitious** glance at his watch during the boring lecture. | The spy made a **surreptitious** exchange of documents in the crowded square. |
| 9 | **Tryst** | Noun | A private, romantic rendezvous between lovers. | They arranged a secret **tryst** at the old lighthouse. | Their forbidden **tryst** was discovered by her jealous husband. | The park bench was a favorite spot for their afternoon **trysts**. |
| 10 | **Voracious** | Adjective | Having a very eager approach to an activity; or, wanting or devouring great quantities of food. | He is a **voracious** reader, finishing a book every two days. | The athlete had a **voracious** appetite after the long training session. | The market has a **voracious** demand for new technology. |
| 11 | **Wistful** | Adjective | Having or showing a feeling of vague or regretful longing. | She gave a **wistful** sigh as she looked at the old photographs. | The song evoked a **wistful** nostalgia for his childhood home. | He had a **wistful** expression as he watched the children play. |
| 12 | **Zealous** | Adjective | Having or showing great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective. | The new employee was incredibly **zealous** and worked late every night. | She is a **zealous** advocate for environmental protection. | His **zealous** support for the team never wavered, even during a losing season. |
| 13 | **Austerity** | Noun | Sternness or severity of manner or attitude; or, difficult economic conditions created by government measures to reduce public expenditure. | The headmaster was known for his strictness and **austerity**. | The country had to adopt a policy of economic **austerity** to reduce its debt. | The monk lived a life of extreme **austerity** and self-denial. |
| 14 | **Capricious** | Adjective | Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. | The weather in the mountains is notoriously **capricious**. | Her **capricious** spending habits often left her short of money. | He found it difficult to work for a boss with such a **capricious** temperament. |
| 15 | **Deleterious** | Adjective | Causing harm or damage. | Smoking has a **deleterious** effect on lung health. | The company's decision had a **deleterious** impact on employee morale. | They are studying the **deleterious** consequences of plastic pollution on marine life. |
| 16 | **Ebullient** | Adjective | Cheerful and full of energy. | The winner was in an **ebullient** mood after receiving the award. | Her **ebullient** personality made her the life of every party. | The team's **ebullient** celebration could be heard across the stadium. |
| 17 | **Fatuous** | Adjective | Silly and pointless. | He made a **fatuous** comment that everyone ignored. | The whole conversation was a waste of time, full of **fatuous** remarks. | She found his constant, **fatuous** grin irritating. |
| 18 | **Garrulous** | Adjective | Excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters. | The **garrulous** taxi driver told me his entire life story during the short ride. | He was so **garrulous** that it was hard to get a word in edgewise. | The old man became more **garrulous** as the evening wore on. |
| 19 | **Hegemony** | Noun | Leadership or dominance, especially by one state or social group over others. | The cultural **hegemony** of Hollywood is felt worldwide. | The small nation struggled to resist the regional **hegemony** of its powerful neighbor. | The company sought to establish market **hegemony** through aggressive acquisitions. |
| 20 | **Inexorable** | Adjective | Impossible to stop or prevent. | The **inexorable** march of time affects us all. | The decline of the old industry seemed **inexorable**. | She faced the **inexorable** truth of her situation with quiet dignity. |
